European Union

CPS4EU Europe

Reliability Research Specialist
2019-2022

During my work on the CPS4EU project, we focused on enhancing the reliability of Cyber-Physical Systems. We established a single flow for both modeling and verification of component-based systems. One of the practical outcomes was devising preventive actions for drone flight collisions by monitoring specific scenarios.To streamline this process, we integrated DR-BIP (Dynamic Reconfigurable Modeling) with THEMIS (Decentralized Monitoring Framework), enabling automated monitoring and further analyzing Gazebo traces of drone simulations. Collaborations with UGA and TUC were instrumental in our project. Additionally, our work intersected with the "Collaborative Lifting" use case of Work Package 8, leading to improved design-time automation and the integration of new tools.

Cyber-Physical Systems WP 5 - Tools Background and Requirement Analysis

EBML Lebanon

Chief Developer
2018

We designed and implemented a distributed web application that collects streams of water consumption data from smart meters. The application processes the data and handles billing while monitoring consumption to generate alerts and notifications. The result is a cutting-edge application deployed in parts of Beirut that when deployed across all of Lebanon has the potential to revolutionize the way water consumption is managed and monitored.

Vehicle Plate Issuance System -- Lebanon

Chief Developer
2017

The application handles the issuance of car plates and RFID stickers, as well as stock management between the government and third-party brokers. The goal of the project was to enhance the efficiency and accuracy of the vehicle plate issuance process by leveraging modern web technologies. The resulting system is a cutting-edge, distributed web application that streamlines the vehicle plate issuance process, making it more efficient and accurate.

TMO Lebanon

Consultant
2015

This project involved working as a consultant and architect for the traffic management office in Lebanon to develop the official TMO mobile application. The app allows citizens to view traffic information in real-time, check cameras of mechanic centers, and check their fees, among other features. The project aimed to improve the efficiency and accessibility of traffic management services in Lebanon.
